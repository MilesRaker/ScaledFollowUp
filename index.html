Problem Statement:

An aircraft is flying horizontally at 100 mph when it powers down its' engines and performs an *ideal* pitch up maneuver, instantly transferring all forward momentum to the +Z direction. What delta-Z will occur between engine cutoff and apogee?

Solution:


Assuming:

  • Gravity is the only force acting on the aircraft
  • Constant gravity of 9.86 m/s^2
  • v = v0 + at
  • delta-z = ((v + v0)/2) * t


Step 1: calculate time to apogee


when does v0 - at = 0?

t = v0/a

t = 100mph/9.86m/s

convert mph -> m/s :

1 miles = 1609 meters

1 hour = 3600 seconds

1 mph = 1609/3600 m/s = 0.447 m/s

v0 = 100 mph * 0.447 = 44.7 m/s

solve for t

t = 44.7 m/s / 9.86m/s^2

t = 4.53s



Step 2: calculate delta-Z


v0 = 44.7 m/s

vfinal = 0m/s

t = 4.53s

delta-Z = ((44.7m/s)/2) * 4.53s

delta-Z = 101m = 331.
